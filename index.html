Privacy Policy for JD Admit

Last updated: April 20, 2026

Overview

JD Admit is a law school application tracking app. This privacy policy explains how we handle information when you use JD Admit.

Information we collect

JD Admit stores all data locally on your device. We collect and store your name, LSAT score, GPA, law school names, deadlines, application notes, and task completion status. We do not transmit or store any of this information on external servers.

AI analysis feature

When you use the AI list analysis feature, your school list data is sent to Anthropic's API to generate personalized advice. This data is governed by Anthropic's privacy policy at anthropic.com/privacy. We do not store AI responses.

In-app purchases

JD Admit offers a one-time Pro upgrade for $2.99, processed through Apple's App Store. We do not collect or store any payment information. All purchases are handled by Apple.

Notifications

With your permission, JD Admit sends local push notifications to remind you of upcoming deadlines. You can disable notifications at any time in your iPhone Settings.

Third-party services

JD Admit uses RevenueCat to manage in-app purchases. RevenueCat may collect device identifiers for purchase verification. See revenuecat.com/privacy for details.

Data storage

All your application data is stored locally on your device. We have no access to your data. If you delete the app, all data is permanently deleted.

Children's privacy

JD Admit is intended for users 17 and older. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13.
